# Scoring Model

Use six dimensions, each scored 0-5. The score is a research prioritization tool, not a price target or trading signal.

| Dimension | 0-1 | 2-3 | 4-5 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Growth | Below industry, shrinking, or one-time growth | Near industry median or mixed quality | Above industry, durable, core-business driven |
| Quality | Weak margins, weak ROIC, unclear model | Average margins and returns | Superior margins, ROIC, unit economics, pricing power |
| Cash flow | Profits do not convert to cash | Inconsistent or cycle-dependent cash flow | Durable FCF, high conversion, strong cash generation |
| Valuation | Expensive relative to growth/quality | Fair, limited margin of safety | Cheap vs peers/history or priced for overly pessimistic assumptions |
| Risk | High leverage, weak liquidity, major cycle/regulatory risk | Manageable but needs monitoring | Strong balance sheet and clear risk controls |
| Sustainability | Weak moat, customer churn, poor execution | Some moat but unproven durability | Strong moat, retention, ecosystem, or management execution |

## Total Score

| Total | Interpretation | Default action |
|---:|---|---|
| 25-30 | High-quality candidate | Worth deeper research and valuation work |
| 20-24 | Attractive but imperfect | Identify the main risk and required margin of safety |
| 15-19 | Ordinary or speculative | Watch only if a catalyst or turnaround thesis exists |
| <15 | Low priority | Usually pass |

## Required Reasoning

Every score should cite concrete facts such as revenue growth, margin, FCF yield, net debt/EBITDA, NRR, ROIC, P/E, EV/EBITDA, P/B, P/FFO, or industry-specific equivalents.

Always distinguish:

- Good company vs good stock.
- Low valuation multiple vs true undervaluation.
- High growth vs high-quality growth.
- Narrative upside vs financial proof.

## Output Rules

- Use a table for scores.
- Include a one-paragraph thesis.
- Include disconfirming evidence.
- Include data freshness and sources.
- Do not output guaranteed buy/sell instructions.
