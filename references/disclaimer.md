# Investment Research Boundaries

Use this language or an equivalent concise form when the user asks for investment conclusions:

This is a research framework, not personalized financial advice. It does not account for the user's full financial situation, tax status, time horizon, liquidity needs, or risk tolerance. Current prices, valuation multiples, earnings, filings, and material news should be verified before any trade.

Avoid:

- "Guaranteed"
- "Risk-free"
- "Must buy"
- "Cannot lose"
- "Perfect stock"
- "This will definitely compound"

Prefer:

- "Worth deeper research"
- "Screens well"
- "Valuation appears reasonable if..."
- "The thesis breaks if..."
- "Position sizing should reflect..."

For high-volatility stocks, explicitly discuss drawdown risk. For banks, insurance, REITs, energy, biotech, and China-related equities, call out the sector-specific risks instead of using generic tech-stock logic.
