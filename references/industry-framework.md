## Universal Process

1. Classify the company by business model.
2. Select only the metrics that work for that industry.
3. Compare with peers, company history, and current rate/cycle context.
4. Ask what pessimistic assumptions current valuation implies.
5. Ask whether future results can disprove those pessimistic assumptions.

## SaaS / Enterprise Software

Examples: SaaS, cloud software, enterprise workflow platforms.

| Dimension | Metrics | Good standard | Risk signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Growth | Revenue growth, ARR growth, customer count | Revenue/ARR >20% good, >30% strong | Growth from acquisitions or one-time large deals |
| Quality | Gross margin, operating margin, Rule of 40 | Gross margin >75%, Rule of 40 >40 | Falling gross margin, excessive SBC |
| Cash flow | FCF margin, FCF conversion | FCF margin >10% good, >20% strong | Profit growth without FCF |
| Valuation | EV/Sales, EV/ARR, FCF yield | Mature companies need FCF yield support | Low EV/Sales caused by collapsing growth |
| Risk | Net cash, SBC, renewal quality | Net cash preferred, SBC ratio declining | Weak retention or sales efficiency |
| Sustainability | NRR, GRR, CAC payback, concentration | NRR >110% good, >120% strong | NRR decline or churn acceleration |

## AI / Cloud / Platform Technology

Examples: cloud platforms, AI platforms, internet platforms, AI infrastructure/application ecosystems.

| Dimension | Metrics | Good standard | Risk signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Growth | Revenue growth, cloud growth, AI revenue | Revenue >15-25% | AI story without revenue contribution |
| Quality | Operating margin, gross margin, ROIC | Operating margin >25%, high ROIC | Capex high with unclear return |
| Cash flow | FCF margin after capex | FCF margin >15-25% | AI capex consumes FCF for too long |
| Valuation | Forward P/E, EV/EBITDA, FCF yield, PEG | PEG <1.5 or FCF yield support | Valuation depends only on distant AI upside |
| Risk | Net cash, capex intensity, regulation | Strong balance sheet preferred | Antitrust, AI cost inflation, platform risk |
| Sustainability | Ecosystem, developer base, data, lock-in | Strong platform/network effects | User stagnation or weak AI monetization |

## Semiconductors / Chip Design

Examples: fabless chip designers, GPUs, CPUs, networking chips, EDA-related chip IP.

| Dimension | Metrics | Good standard | Risk signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Growth | Revenue CAGR, orders, design wins | Revenue CAGR >10-15% | One cycle drives all growth |
| Quality | Gross margin, operating margin, ROIC | Gross margin >50%, operating margin >25% | Margin decline, inventory build |
| Cash flow | FCF margin, inventory turnover | FCF margin >15% | Inventory/receivables outgrow revenue |
| Valuation | Forward P/E, EV/EBITDA, PEG | Below cycle median with intact growth | Low P/E at cycle peak |
| Risk | Customer concentration, supply chain | Healthy inventory and diversified demand | Large customer cuts orders |
| Sustainability | IP, ecosystem, process, software stack | High switching costs and product cadence | Technology route is displaced |

## Semiconductor Equipment / Hardware

Examples: semiconductor equipment, servers, networking equipment, industrial hardware.

| Dimension | Metrics | Good standard | Risk signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Growth | Revenue growth, backlog, orders | Revenue >8-12% | Backlog rolls over |
| Quality | Operating margin, ROIC | Operating margin >20% | Price competition compresses margin |
| Cash flow | FCF margin, working capital | FCF margin >10-15% | Receivables/inventory consume cash |
| Valuation | EV/EBITDA, forward P/E | Below history with healthy orders | Cheap because order cycle is turning down |
| Risk | Net debt/EBITDA, concentration | Net debt/EBITDA <2x | Single customer/region risk |
| Sustainability | Technical barriers, service base | Service revenue share rises | Hardware commoditizes |

## Consumer Brands / Staples

| Dimension | Metrics | Good standard | Risk signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Growth | Organic growth, volume, pricing | Organic growth >3-6% | Growth only from price while volume falls |
| Quality | Gross margin, operating margin, ROIC | Operating margin >15-25% | More promotion, margin decline |
| Cash flow | FCF conversion, dividend cover | FCF conversion >90% | Cash flow below earnings |
| Valuation | P/E, dividend yield, FCF yield | Discount to peers with stable growth | Cheap because brand is aging |
| Risk | Debt, interest cover, input costs | Interest coverage >6x | High leverage and commodity shock |
| Sustainability | Brand, channel, pricing power | Can raise price without losing share | Private-label or demographic erosion |

## Retail / E-commerce

| Dimension | Metrics | Good standard | Risk signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Growth | Same-store sales, GMV, orders | Same-store sales >3% | Store growth masks weak unit economics |
| Quality | Gross margin, operating margin, inventory turns | Operating margin >5-10% | Inventory build and discounting |
| Cash flow | FCF margin, working capital | FCF margin >3-8% | Inventory absorbs cash |
| Valuation | EV/EBITDA, P/E, FCF yield | Discount with stable same-store sales | Cheap due to structural traffic decline |
| Risk | Lease liabilities, inventory, cash | Healthy inventory turns | Lease burden or price war |
| Sustainability | Repeat rate, fulfillment cost, supply chain | Repeat high and fulfillment cost falling | Customer acquisition cost rises |

## Industrials / Manufacturing

| Dimension | Metrics | Good standard | Risk signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Growth | Revenue growth, orders, backlog | Revenue >5-10% | Orders from cycle peak |
| Quality | Operating margin, ROIC | Operating margin >10-18%, ROIC >10-15% | Margin overly sensitive to utilization |
| Cash flow | FCF conversion, capex | FCF conversion >80% | High capex with low return |
| Valuation | EV/EBITDA, P/E | Below cycle median with stable orders | Cheap as downcycle begins |
| Risk | Net debt/EBITDA, interest cover | Net debt/EBITDA <2.5x | High leverage or cancellations |
| Sustainability | Technology, service revenue, customer stickiness | Service revenue rises | Product commoditization |

## Energy / Oil and Gas

| Dimension | Metrics | Good standard | Risk signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Growth | Production, reserves, projects | Capital returns matter more than growth | Blind expansion |
| Quality | ROCE, unit cost, reserve replacement | High ROCE, low breakeven oil price | Costs rise, reserves fall |
| Cash flow | FCF yield, shareholder returns | FCF yield >8-12% attractive | No FCF despite high commodity prices |
| Valuation | EV/EBITDA, P/NAV, FCF yield | Cheap with capital discipline | Cheap at commodity cycle peak |
| Risk | Net debt, maturities, hedges | Low debt and stable returns | High leverage, high-cost assets |
| Sustainability | Cost curve, reserve life, discipline | Low-cost, long reserve life | ESG, regulatory, impairment risk |

## Banks

Do not use EV/EBITDA for banks.

| Dimension | Metrics | Good standard | Risk signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Growth | Loan growth, deposit growth, fee income | Loan growth >3-7% | Credit quality deteriorates |
| Quality | ROE, ROA, NIM, efficiency ratio | ROE >12%, ROA >1% | NIM shrinks, NPL rises |
| Cash flow | Capital generation, dividends | CET1 stable, payout sustainable | Capital shortfall |
| Valuation | P/B, P/TBV, P/E | P/B <1 only attractive if ROE can improve | Low P/B because asset quality is bad |
| Risk | CET1, NPL, reserves | Strong capital and low NPL | Deposit flight, CRE exposure |
| Sustainability | Core deposits, relationships, risk culture | Stable low-cost deposits | Wholesale funding dependence |

## Payment Networks / Financial Infrastructure

Examples: card networks, payment processors, merchant acquirers, and financial data rails.

| Dimension | Metrics | Good standard | Risk signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Growth | Payment volume, transactions, cross-border volume, revenue | Revenue growth >8-12% good, >15% strong | Growth only from pricing, transaction growth slows |
| Quality | Operating margin, take rate, ROIC | Operating margin >40%, very high FCF conversion | Margin compression from pricing/regulation |
| Cash flow | FCF margin, FCF conversion, buybacks | FCF margin >25%, FCF conversion >80% | Cash flow lags earnings or buybacks mask dilution |
| Valuation | Forward P/E, EV/EBITDA, FCF yield | Reasonable if growth and margin durability persist | Multiple assumes no regulatory pressure |
| Risk | Net debt/EBITDA, litigation/regulation, cyber | Net debt/EBITDA <2x, resilient balance sheet | Fee caps, antitrust, network bypass risk |
| Sustainability | Network effects, acceptance footprint, issuer/merchant relationships | Two-sided network and global acceptance are hard to replicate | Real-time payments, stablecoins, or local rails weaken the moat |

## Insurance

| Dimension | Metrics | Good standard | Risk signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Growth | Premium growth, new business value | Premium growth >5-10% | Growth sacrifices underwriting |
| Quality | Combined ratio, ROE | Combined ratio <95%, ROE >10-15% | Loss ratio rises |
| Cash flow | Underwriting profit, investment income | Stable underwriting and investment engine | Yield from risky assets |
| Valuation | P/B, P/E, embedded value | Discount relative to ROE | Reserves understated |
| Risk | Solvency, reserves, duration matching | Strong solvency and ALM | Rate/reserve mismatch |
| Sustainability | Underwriting discipline, distribution, brand | Combined ratio stable over cycles | Price war |

## Utilities

| Dimension | Metrics | Good standard | Risk signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Growth | EPS growth, rate base growth | EPS growth >4-7% | Growth from leverage |
| Quality | ROE, regulated return | Stable allowed ROE | Regulated return cut |
| Cash flow | FFO/debt, dividend cover | Healthy FFO/debt | Dividend exceeds cash capacity |
| Valuation | P/E, EV/EBITDA, dividend yield | Yield attractive vs rates | High yield with no growth |
| Risk | Debt/EBITDA, rate sensitivity | Manageable maturities | Refinancing pressure |
| Sustainability | Regulation, asset base | Rate base grows predictably | Policy or project-delay risk |

## REITs

Do not rely on EPS for REITs. Prefer FFO/AFFO, NAV, occupancy, and leverage.

| Dimension | Metrics | Good standard | Risk signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Growth | NOI growth, FFO growth, rent growth | NOI/FFO growth >3-6% | Rent growth from short-lived supply crunch |
| Quality | Occupancy, lease term, same-store NOI | High occupancy, strong tenants | Occupancy falls |
| Cash flow | AFFO, payout cover | AFFO payout <80% | Dividend exceeds AFFO |
| Valuation | P/FFO, NAV discount, dividend yield | P/FFO below peers with quality assets | Discount reflects impairment |
| Risk | Net debt/EBITDA, maturities | Net debt/EBITDA <6x | Short debt and rate sensitivity |
| Sustainability | Location, supply/demand, tenant quality | Good locations and stable tenants | Structural property decline |

## Biotech / Drug Development

Traditional P/E is usually not useful for clinical-stage biotech.

| Dimension | Metrics | Good standard | Risk signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Growth | Clinical progress, TAM, product revenue | Clear clinical milestones and large market | TAM story without clinical proof |
| Quality | Pipeline quality, probability, patents | Multiple assets and clear mechanism | Single-asset binary risk |
| Cash flow | Cash runway | Runway >18-24 months | Frequent financing needed |
| Valuation | EV / risk-adjusted pipeline value | EV below risk-adjusted value | Cheap because trials likely fail |
| Risk | Cash, dilution, FDA risk | Cash sufficient and dilution controlled | Regulatory or patent challenge |
| Sustainability | Platform, BD, patent life | Repeatable platform and long protection | One-time asset only |

## Universal Red Flags

- Revenue grows while receivables grow much faster.
- Earnings grow while FCF does not follow.
- Gross margin or operating margin deteriorates for multiple periods.
- Management changes definitions or leans heavily on non-GAAP without clarity.
- SBC remains high without a declining trend.
- Customer concentration is high without renewal/contract quality disclosure.
- High leverage meets cycle downturn.
- A low valuation reflects structural decline rather than mispricing.
- The investment case requires perfect execution five years out with no interim validation points.
